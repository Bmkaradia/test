FREQUENT QUESTIONS

Why “Isbel?”

Our name Is-bel is a combination of the word “is” and the Italian word for beautiful – bella. We believe each and every woman is a beautiful, radiant creature – she ‘is bella.’ We want it to remind you of your own inner beauty every time you see our name. Think of it like an ultra-mini mantra.

Why orange?

A lot of men and women expected us to go with pink – the quintessential girl color – or something dark and smoky like purple, grey, or black. But we didn't like that these colors promote sensuality as dark or shameful, and come from media that typically portrays women as sexual objects, not sexual beings in their own right.  Our palette of warm, peach and tangerine tones mimic the colors you might see in a gentle sunrise, soft and radiant.  We wanted to be about how women feel when they are at their best sensual selves - glowing, comfortable in their skin, and fully alive.

Is this…porn?

Nope, nope, and nope! Our site is 100% SFW (safe for work). Anything we post on Isbel would be equally at home in an art gallery. You won’t find any explicit nudity, shocking material, or explicit videos and photographs here. Isbel is entirely about intelligent conversation, connection, and learning.

Why women only?

The existing context for the social conversation about female sex and sensuality is a bit skewed. It’s still very much centered around the men, while we still know comparatively little about how women experience intimacy and growth as sensual creatures.

How private is ‘private?’

Private with a capital ‘P’. Isbel is completely anonymous – we will never ask to post for you to any social network or even link your Isbel account to your friends, and your data is always fiercly protected.

I’m a guy. Can I join Isbel?

Isbel is just for women, but men are an important part of the intimacy picture! Nurturing open communication among partners is one of our primary goals at Isbel. We encourage women to bring their partners in on the conversation. But opening our hearts and minds is easier said than done. If you’re looking for insight, we invite you to join our email newsletter or follow us on Tumblr.
